Open Data Standard for All (UK) Nonprofits

OpenVCS

OCVA is the first "VCS" or "CVS" to publish open data for the voluntary, community, charity and social enterprise (non-profit) sector. We're calling our format OpenVCS and we are in dialogue about organisations like OCVA across the UK publishing their own data to this (or at least a shared standard) format.

Standards are the Key

We're not precious about our own 'OpenVCS' format. It's just a set of fields in a spreadsheet for exporting and publishing data. With open data what's more important is having a shared standard. But we're ambitious; we think peer voluntary and community associations across the UK could share a standard together. It could be ours, or an evolution of ours. Or something else. We offer our format as a proposed standard-in-progress. If everyone publishes to a standard, things can be done with it and it starts to become intelligence.

About our Format

Our format is derived from CiviCRM and uses the following fields. Replace {AREA} with county or city name (for example, Oxfordshire) and {REGION} with UK region (for example South East Region):

Company Number For registered companies

Average Annual Turnover Choose from: Less than £5,000 (A); £5,000 - £24,999 (B); £25,000 - £49,999 (B); £50,000 - £99,000 (B); £100,000 - £249,000 (B2); £250,000 - £499,000 (C); £500,000 - £999,000 (C); £1million or more (C)

Geographic Areas Service is Provided Choose from: {AREA} City, {AREA} District(s), {AREA} and adjacent counties, {REGION}, UK, Outside UK
Wards covered in {AREA} A list of ward codes in the 2011 census format. (Example Cherwell E05006517 Adderbury). Includes option for 'ALL'.
Geo Code 1 Latitude or Longitude numeric. Derived from postcode (not shared).*
Geo Code 2 Latitude or Longitude numeric. Derived from postcode (not shared).*
Disbanded Date range in the format DD/MM/YYYY
*Groups must provide explicit opt-in to be included in our data set. Upon joining OCVA or filling out a data submission form, groups are told that postcodes are used to provide map point data. If they wish, they can provide a slightly altered postcode. We are always on hand to help groups to do this.
